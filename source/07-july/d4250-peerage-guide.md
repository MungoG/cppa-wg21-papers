## Abstract

WG21 has fifty-seven officer positions. This paper records who holds each one, when they were appointed, and what public evidence documents the appointment. It also records whether any position has undergone the national body confirmation the ISO/IEC Directives prescribe for working group leadership.

## 1. Disclosure

The author provides information and serves at the pleasure of the committee.

The author is affiliated with the C++ Alliance. This paper documents the tenure of WG21 officers against the governance framework established by the ISO/IEC Directives. Appointment dates are derived from public meeting minutes, admin telecon minutes, and published conference biographies. Undocumented internal processes are not accessible to the author.

This paper is a companion to [P4196R0](https://wg21.link/p4196r0)<sup>[1]</sup> ("Discrepancies Between SD-4 and the ISO Directives").

This paper asks for nothing.

---

## 2. Functional Equivalence

[P4196R0](https://wg21.link/p4196r0)<sup>[1]</sup> Section 2.1 establishes that WG21's subgroups satisfy every functional criterion the ISO/IEC Directives assign to a working group while exceeding them in gatekeeping authority. [P4196R0](https://wg21.link/p4196r0)<sup>[1]</sup> Section 7 addresses the counterargument that Directive 1.12.1 applies only to working group convenors. This paper proceeds from that established equivalence and provides the empirical data.

---

## 3. Two Provisions

The ISO/IEC Directives Part 1<sup>[2]</sup>, clause 1.12.1:

> "Working group Convenors shall be appointed by the committee for up to three-year terms. Such appointments shall be confirmed by the National Body (or liaison). The Convenor may be reappointed for additional terms of up to three years. There is no limit to the number of terms."

SD-4<sup>[3]</sup>:

> "Subgroup chairs are appointed by the convener, and are selected to match the current needs of the subgroup. They have no fixed term."

---

## 4. The WG21 Convener

The national body confirmation mechanism has been applied to the WG21 convener role. SC22 Resolution 99-T (1999)<sup>[4]</sup> records the reappointment of Tom Plum as WG21 convener "pending confirmation from the USA Member Body." In 2025, Guy Davidson was appointed WG21 convener through the SC22 plenary process with national body nominations<sup>[5]</sup>. The mechanism exists. It works. It has been applied at this level.

## 6. Methodology

Appointment dates were determined from the following public sources, in order of preference:

1. WG21 Minutes of Meeting papers (N-series) recording formal announcements of chair transitions at opening or closing plenary
2. Admin telecon minutes (N-series) recording leadership listings per subgroup
3. Convener trip reports and announcements on herbsutter.com
4. Published conference speaker biographies with dated role descriptions
5. Personal CVs and professional profiles with dated role entries
6. The isocpp.org committee page as a source of last resort for positions with no other public record

Where the exact appointment date is undocumented, the earliest and latest admin telecon minutes that bracket the transition are cited, and the tenure is computed from the midpoint. Transitions described as "gradual" (no single announcement) are noted.

This methodology captures publicly documented evidence. The author acknowledges that private correspondence, informal agreements, or undocumented internal processes may exist. The paper records what is findable, not what is knowable.
